    PARLIAMENT HOUSE:

    Parliament House Islamabad, also known as the National Assembly Building, is the legislative
    seat of the Government of Pakistan. It is located in the capital city of Islamabad, which was
    purpose-built to replace Karachi as Pakistan's capital in the 1960s. The Parliament House is an
    iconic and important landmark in Pakistan's political landscape. Here are some key details about
    Parliament House Islamabad:


    Architecture:
    Parliament House Islamabad was designed by the famous Greek architect and town
    planner, Constantinos Apostolou Doxiadis. The building's architecture is a blend of modern and
    Islamic influences, with its unique shape resembling a traditional Mughal tent. It features a
    dome and extensive use of marble, reflecting a sense of grandeur and importance.

    Location:
    The Parliament House is situated at the western end of the Constitution Avenue, which is also
    home to many other government buildings and embassies in Islamabad. The building's
    prominent location symbolizes its central role in Pakistan's democratic processes.

    National Assembly:
    The National Assembly of Pakistan is the lower house of the bicameral Parliament of Pakistan.
    It consists of elected representatives from all over Pakistan, with the number of seats allocated
    to each province based on its population. The National Assembly plays a vital role in the
    legislative process and the formation of the federal government.
