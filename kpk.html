POPULATION OF KPK :

As of my last knowledge update in September 2021, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK) is a province in
Pakistan. At that time, the population of KPK was estimated to be around 35 million people.
However, please note that population figures can change over time due to various factors
including births, deaths, and migrations. For the most up-to-date and accurate population
information, I recommend referring to official sources such as the Pakistan
Bureau of Statistics or other reliable demographic sources.

CALTURE :

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK) is a province in Pakistan with a rich and diverse cultural
heritage. The culture of KPK is deeply rooted in its history, traditions, and the
lifestyle of its people. Here are some key aspects of KPK's culture:

Pakhtunwali:
Pakhtunwali is the traditional ethical code and way of life followed by the Pashtun people in KPK.
It emphasizes concepts like hospitality (melmastia), courage (tora), loyalty (wafa), and justice
(adalat). It also includes concepts of revenge (badal) and forgiveness (nanawatai). Pakhtunwali
plays a significant role in shaping the behavior and values of the people in KPK.

Language:
The primary language spoken in KPK is Pashto, which holds a central place in the culture and
identity of the region. Pashto poetry and literature have a long history and are highly
regarded among the Pashtun people.

Dress
The traditional dress for Pashtun men is the shalwar kameez, often paired with a waistcoat or a
traditional vest. For women, the traditional attire includes the kameez (long tunic)
and shalwar (loose pants), often paired with a dupatta (scarf).
